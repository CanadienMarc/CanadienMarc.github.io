About Computer Science and Robotics

What will I learn?

In computer science you will learn how to program in Java, Android and HTML5. With these programing languages you will be able to program apps on your cell phone/tablet, virtual reality headsets and develope your own website. What you learn here at Queen Elizabeth High School is the same knowledge as a second year university student.

In Robotics you will learn about electrical robotic systems, and subsystems that explore different areas. Robotics at Queen Elizabeth High School allows students to go from having no robotics experience, to programming advanced behaviors.

What is the fun?

Welcome to the fun side of computer science and robotics! At Queen Elizabeth you will explore how computer science and robotics are also about people, solving puzzles, creativity, changing the future and, most of all, having fun. Computer science and robotics are peer driven, so that means that you work with your friends to create programs, robots, or whatever your heart desires. By being part of this program, you will feel like God by building stuff that stays around forever. Being a computer scientist means that you can talk to the computer in its own language and make it do what you want it to do, which ultimately means that you are golden

Opportunities

There are major benefits to taking computer science and/or robotics at Queen Elizabeth. You may find yourself excelling in other areas such as problem solving and working as a team. Choosing to be a computer scientist as a career is a great choice because software is where the jobs are at. Just take a look around you, technology is everywhere and it's growing fast! We need creative minds like your own. By the time you leave high school, you will already know enough to become a professional in your area of interest. So why not be a part of the future? Besides, it is a wonderful feeling when you make your idea into reality.

Assessments

  • Quizzes are plus minus (used for memorizing information), not counted
  • Literacy DOC is graded at various times through the term
  • Hackathon type exams form one type of graded assessment
  • Projects form the greatest assessment
  • Multiple written exams are saved for CS30, AP-CSA, and APCSP (used for post-secondary entrance and skipping 1st year post-secondary courses, $$$)
