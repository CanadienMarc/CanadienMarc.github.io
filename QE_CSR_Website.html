About Computer Science and Robotics

What will I learn?

In computer science you will learn how to program in Java, Android and HTML5. With these programing languages you will be able to program apps on your cell phone/tablet, virtual reality headsets and develope your own website. What you learn here at Queen Elizabeth High School is the same knowledge as a second year university student.
In Robotics you will learn about electrical robotic systems, and subsystems that explore different areas. Robotics at Queen Elizabeth High School allows students to go from having no robotics experience, to programming advanced behaviors.

What is the fun?

Welcome to the fun side of computer science and robotics! At Queen Elizabeth you will explore how computer science and robotics are also about people, solving puzzles, creativity, changing the future and, most of all, having fun. Computer science and robotics are peer driven, so that means that you work with your friends to create programs, robots, or whatever your heart desires. By being part of this program, you will feel like God by building stuff that stays around forever. Being a computer scientist means that you can talk to the computer in its own language and make it do what you want it to do, which ultimately means that you are golden.

Opportunities

There are major benefits to taking computer science and/or robotics at Queen Elizabeth. You may find yourself excelling in other areas such as problem solving and working as a team.   5. General Steps
    1. Prepare the SD Card (Click Here)

      2. Preparing the SD Card

      1. 1Download the three software pieces above.
      2. Take your microSD card out of the Raspberry Pi, put it into the microSD card reader, hook that up to your computer (USB Drive).
      3. Notice the drive letter of your microSD Card.
      4. Use the SD card formatter and format the drive (microSD Card).
        No need for special options just use the defaults
        Note, there’s a chance that the capacity of the drive will show up wrong before you format it, don’t worry about it, formatting the card will fix the capacity issue.

        If you have done a lot of work with your SD Card, Mr. Mercer says the formatting might fail by only erasing the reference table. You will need to do a complete erase. Please talk with him about the details.
      5. 1. Once the SD card has been formatted, put it to the side.
    2. Writing the unzipped image to the SD Card (Click Here)

      3. Writing the New Image to the microSD Card (drive)

      • Extract the disk image out of the .gz file that was downloaded from the Retropie website.
        • .gz is like a .tar file used like a ZIP file in Linux
        • “Unzip” this file using 7-Zip or WinRAR, or another program of your choice.
        • Stop when you see the .img file.
      • Once the image has been extracted note it’s pathway or system of folders
      • Use Win32DiskImager to write the image to the SD card.

        Remember: to right click to open but RUN AS ADMINISTRATOR or similar on your machine
        There will be a few pop up messages warning that this could corrupt the device, this is fine, continue on and press OK if you get these messages.

      • Ensure the Pathway is correct – where the file is coming from.
        Ensure the drive is the correct letter as the microSD Card.
        Press the WRITE Button.
      • Once the image is written to the SD card, dismount the drive (safely remove or eject the microSD card reader) and put the microSD card back into the Raspberry Pi.
    3. Getting your RPi going at Home(Click Here)

      4. Connecting your RPi at home

      1. Hook up the Raspberry Pi to a TV or monitor.
        • You will need a keyboard; mouse isn’t necessary but nice if you like the GUI.
        • An internet connection is required for some of the steps ahead, an ethernet cable is recommended because I experienced issues trying to connect over WiFi, though I will include how to connect to WiFi in the steps
        • I also recommend having a wired controller (xbox 360, ps4, third party) hooked up. This is not needed since the keyboard will do.
      2. Once the Raspberry Pi is hooked up let it do it’s first time boot up. This could take a minute or two so be patient.
      3. Once the first-time-setup is finished you will see a screen that asks for controller input, hold any button on your controller or keyboard to set up controls for it.
      4. Assign the controls accordingly. The ones you want to pay attention to are the ‘start’, ‘a’ and ‘d-pad’ keys, as these keys will be used to navigate the menus. You can assign nothing to a key by holding down any key when it asks you to input one. You can also reassign the keys from the start menu if you are unhappy with your selection later.
      5. Once you have finished selecting your controls navigate to the Retropie options section (by default it should be the icon you start on, it looks very similar to the Retropie boot up screen).
      6. If you are connected to the internet through wifi: (Skip this step if you are connected to the internet through ethernet cable)
        Once there go down to the option that let’s you set up WiFi. A BIOS looking screen will pop up, from here you need to use the arrow keys on your keyboard and the enter button, a controller will not work on these screens. Navigate to your WiFi network and input the proper password and connect. I had a couple of issues connecting even when I was inputting the proper password, if this happens to you wait five minutes and try again and or reset your router. If the problems persist then see if you can get a hold of an ethernet cable.
      7. Once we are connected to the internet navigate back to the main Retropie options menu. From here we will go down to the RaspPi options. We come to another BIOS looking screen. We don’t really want to mess with any of the options here except for one, go down to the advanced options and then select memory allocation. Here we want to input 256. We want to back out to the main Retropie options menu.
    4. Installing Emulators and ROMs (Click Here)

      5. Installing Emulators and ROMs:

      1. Once back at the menu select the RetroPie setup option. Here we come to our third and final ‘BIOS’ screen. Go down to where it says install individual emulators from binary or source. Go through the list and install anything that strikes your fancy, just make sure to install the binaries and not the source, the source can take 3 times as long to install.
      2. Once we have all of our emulators installed, back out of the BIOS menus and go back to the main menu, it’s time to load up our Raspberry Pi with ROMs.
        • You need a USB for this step, it doesn’t need to be big (1GB will do) unless you’re loading PSX games.
        • First format the USB by putting it into your computer or laptop and right clicking the drive in my computer and selecting the format option. Make sure to remove anything valuable from the USB before you format it.
        • Once it’s formatted plug it into the Raspberry pi and leave it alone for 30 seconds or so, or until the light on the USB (if it has one) stops blinking.
        • Plug the USB back into the computer and there should be a folder named rom in there will be other folders that have the name of different system (NES, SNES, n64, PSX, etc). Put your ROMs into their proper folders within the USB (n64 ROMs in n64 folder).
        • To transfer the ROMs into the Raspberry Pi, you need to put the USB back into the Raspberry Pi and wait until the light stops blinking, it will blink in intervals so make sure that it doesn’t blink for a good minute before you remove it. If your USB has no light then just wait 5 to 15 minutes (it depends on how much you put onto the USB) before removing it.
        • Once the USB has transferred over the ROMs take your controller or keyboard and press the start button (or whatever you assigned it to on the keyboard) and go to quit, there it will have a couple options, chose the one that says “restart emulation station.” Once the software has rebooted your ROMs should be there and ready to play.
    5. Play and Enjoy!
